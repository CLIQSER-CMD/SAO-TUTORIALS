Cybersecurity Career Roadmap

A guide to starting and advancing your career in the exciting and in-demand field of cybersecurity.

Why Pursue a Career in Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is one of the fastest-growing fields in technology. With a massive talent shortage and increasing demand, a career in cybersecurity offers job security, competitive salaries, and the opportunity to do meaningful work protecting people and organizations from cyber threats.

Phase 1: Foundational Skills

Networking Fundamentals

Understand the basics of how networks work, including TCP/IP, DNS, and HTTP/S.

Operating Systems

Get comfortable with both Windows and Linux environments, especially the command line.

Programming/Scripting

Learn a scripting language like Python for automation and a compiled language like C or Go to understand how software works at a low level.

Phase 2: Entry-Level Certifications and Roles

Once you have the foundational skills, you can start looking at entry-level certifications and job roles.

Entry-Level Certifications

  • CompTIA Security+: A great starting point that covers a wide range of security topics.
  • Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH): Focuses on the tools and techniques of ethical hacking.

Entry-Level Roles

  • Security Analyst: Monitors security alerts and investigates incidents.
  • Junior Penetration Tester: Looks for vulnerabilities in systems and applications.
  • IT Support with a Security Focus: Helps users with security-related issues.

Phase 3: Specializations

As you gain experience, you can specialize in a particular area of cybersecurity.

  • Offensive Security (Red Team): Penetration testing, vulnerability research, exploit development.
  • Defensive Security (Blue Team): Incident response, threat hunting, digital forensics.
  • Cloud Security: Securing cloud environments like AWS, Azure, and GCP.
  • Application Security: Securing web and mobile applications.
