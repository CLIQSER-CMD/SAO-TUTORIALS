How to Spot Fake / Phishing Websites

Learn to identify red flags and protect yourself from malicious websites designed to steal your personal information.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals create fake websites that look identical to legitimate ones (like your bank, social media, or email provider) to trick you into entering your login credentials, credit card numbers, or other sensitive information.

Why Phishing is Dangerous

  • ‚Ä¢ 90% of data breaches start with phishing attacks
  • ‚Ä¢ Phishing sites can look identical to real ones
  • ‚Ä¢ One compromised account can lead to identity theft
  • ‚Ä¢ Financial losses from phishing average $4,000+ per incident

Prevention Best Practices

Before Clicking Links

  • ‚Ä¢ Hover over links to preview the URL
  • ‚Ä¢ Check for HTTPS and valid certificates
  • ‚Ä¢ Type URLs manually instead of clicking
  • ‚Ä¢ Use bookmarks for important sites
  • ‚Ä¢ Be wary of shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl)

When in Doubt

  • ‚Ä¢ Contact the company directly
  • ‚Ä¢ Use official apps instead of browsers
  • ‚Ä¢ Enable two-factor authentication
  • ‚Ä¢ Report suspicious emails and sites
  • ‚Ä¢ Trust your instincts‚Äîif it feels off, it probably is

Helpful Tools and Resources

Browser Security Features

Modern browsers have built-in phishing protection. Keep them updated and pay attention to security warnings.

Password Managers

Password managers can detect and warn about phishing sites by comparing URLs to stored credentials.

Security Extensions

Consider browser extensions like HTTPS Everywhere and uBlock Origin for additional protection.

What to Do If You Think You've Been Phished

Immediate Actions

  1. 1. Disconnect from the internet immediately
  2. 2. Change your passwords for the compromised account and any others using the same password
  3. 3. Enable two-factor authentication on all affected accounts
  4. 4. Contact your bank if you entered financial information
  5. 5. Run a malware scan on your device
  6. 6. Report the phishing attempt to the legitimate company
