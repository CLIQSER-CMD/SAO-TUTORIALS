Scam Awareness Guide

Learn to identify and protect yourself from common online scams, from phishing to fake job offers.

The Pervasiveness of Online Scams

Scams are more sophisticated than ever, and anyone can be a target. This guide will equip you with the knowledge to recognize the red flags of common online scams and protect yourself and your finances.

Common Types of Scams

Phishing Scams

Emails, texts, or messages that appear to be from a legitimate company, asking you to provide sensitive information.

Tech Support Scams

Pop-up messages or calls claiming your computer is infected with a virus, urging you to pay for unnecessary technical support.

Fake Job Offers

Scammers pose as recruiters for real companies and may ask for personal information or payment for "training" or "equipment".

Investment Scams

Promises of high returns with little or no risk. Often involve cryptocurrency or "secret" investment strategies.

Red Flags of a Scam

  • Sense of Urgency: Pressure to act immediately.
  • Unsolicited Contact: You're contacted out of the blue.
  • Request for Personal Information: Asking for passwords, Social Security numbers, or bank details.
  • Unusual Payment Methods: Requesting payment via gift cards, wire transfers, or cryptocurrency.
  • Too Good to Be True: Offers that seem unbelievable usually are.
  • Poor Grammar and Spelling: Professional companies usually have high-quality communications.

How to Protect Yourself

The best defense against scams is a healthy dose of skepticism and good security hygiene.

  • Verify, Verify, Verify: If you receive an unsolicited request, contact the company or person directly using a known email address or phone number.
  • Don't Click on Suspicious Links: Hover over links to see the actual URL. If it looks suspicious, don't click it.
  • Secure Your Accounts: Use strong, unique passwords and enable 2FA on all your important accounts.
  • Think Before You Act: Scammers rely on impulse. Take a moment to think before you respond or provide information.
