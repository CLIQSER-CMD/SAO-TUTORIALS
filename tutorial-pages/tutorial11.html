How to Detect Malware on Your Phone/PC

Learn to recognize the signs of malware infection on your devices and the steps to take for removal and prevention.

What is Malware?

Malware, short for malicious software, is any software intentionally designed to cause disruption to a computer, server, client, or computer network, leak private information, gain unauthorized access to information or systems, deprive users access to information or which unknowingly interferes with the user's computer security and privacy.

Common Types of Malware

  • Viruses: Attach to clean files and spread to other clean files.
  • Worms: Spread across networks without any user interaction.
  • Trojans: Disguise themselves as legitimate software.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts your files and demands a ransom for the decryption key.
  • Spyware: Secretly observes the user's activities.
  • Adware: Automatically delivers advertisements.

Common Symptoms of Malware Infection

Unusual Slowness

Your device runs significantly slower than usual, applications take a long time to load, or it freezes frequently.

Unexpected Pop-ups and Ads

You see a sudden increase in pop-up ads, even when you're not browsing the internet.

Rapid Battery Drain (Phones)

Your phone's battery drains much faster than normal, even with light usage.

High Data Usage

You notice a sudden, unexplained spike in your internet or mobile data usage.

Ransom Messages

You see a message demanding payment to unlock your files or device.

How to Detect Malware on a PC (Windows/macOS)

Step 1: Run a Malware Scan

Use a reputable antivirus or anti-malware program to scan your system. Popular choices include Malwarebytes, Bitdefender, and Windows Defender.

Step 2: Check for Unfamiliar Applications

Look through your installed applications list for any software you don't recognize. Uninstall any suspicious programs.

Step 3: Monitor Network Activity

Use tools like Task Manager (Windows) or Activity Monitor (macOS) to see if any unknown processes are using a lot of network resources.

Step 4: Check Browser Extensions

Review your web browser's extensions and remove any that you did not install or that look suspicious.

How to Detect Malware on a Phone (Android/iOS)

Step 1: Look for Suspicious Apps

Check your app list for any apps you don't remember installing. Also, check app permissions to see if any have excessive access to your data.

Step 2: Monitor Data and Battery Usage

In your phone's settings, check which apps are using the most data and battery. Unusually high usage from an unknown app is a major red flag.

Step 3: Run a Mobile Security Scan

Install a mobile security app from a trusted brand (like Avast, Bitdefender, or Malwarebytes) and run a full scan.

Step 4: Check for Unexplained Charges

Review your phone bill for any unexpected charges, which could be a sign of malware sending premium SMS messages.

Removal Checklist

  • 1. Enter Safe Mode: Reboot your device into Safe Mode to prevent non-essential apps (and most malware) from running.
  • 2. Run a Malware Scan: Use your security software to run a full system scan and remove any detected threats.
  • 3. Uninstall Suspicious Apps: Manually remove any applications you don't recognize.
  • 4. Clear Browser Cache: Clear your browser's cache and reset its settings to default.
  • 5. Change Your Passwords: After cleaning your device, change the passwords for your important accounts (email, banking, social media).
  • 6. Factory Reset (Last Resort): If the infection persists, a full factory reset is the most effective way to ensure all malware is gone. Make sure to back up your important files first.
